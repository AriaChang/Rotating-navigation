Why I started crafting

I began crafting in 2020 when Covid-19 swept the world. At that time, I desperately needed a job, but due to the faltering economy, finding one was challenging. I believe many can relate to that period. However, my feelings of depression didn't start then. They began when I came to the USA to study in 2014. As a foreigner in a new country, I was excited to learn and had set ambitious goals for myself. Over time, I began to feel pressure as I encountered numerous obstacles during my experiences in the USA. These challenges extended beyond academics and included finding accommodation, meeting familial expectations, navigating visa requirements, seeking employment, adjusting to a different culture, coping with loneliness and language barriers, and even the simple task of scheduling appointments presented difficulties. In 2020, I discovered embroidery, initially not expecting to develop such a keen interest in crafting, despite my sister's enthusiasm for it. I was initially skeptical, but upon trying it myself, I found it captivating. It felt almost magical, and I found myself eager to practice it daily, experimenting with various techniques, textures, and tools, eagerly anticipating the final result.

It's both magical and therapeutic.

As I delve deeper into crafting, I find that I'm channeling my stress into handmade creations, and I feel a sense of fulfillment every time I complete a piece of art. I feel satisfied and happy. Surprisingly, I spend up to 12 hours a day crafting. So, I thought, why not open a store to share this passion with everyone?
